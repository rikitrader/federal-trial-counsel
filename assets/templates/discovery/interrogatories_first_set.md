                              DEFINITIONS

     1. "You," "Your," or "{{RESPONDING_PARTY}}" means {{FULL_NAME}},
including all agents, employees, representatives, attorneys, and any
other persons acting on behalf of {{RESPONDING_PARTY}}.

     2. "Document" means any written, printed, typed, recorded, or graphic
matter of any kind, or any other tangible thing, including but not limited
to: correspondence, memoranda, notes, reports, agreements, contracts,
communications, emails, text messages, instant messages, social media
posts, photographs, recordings, spreadsheets, databases, and electronically
stored information (ESI) as defined by FRCP 34(a).

     3. "Communication" means any oral, written, or electronic exchange of
information, including but not limited to letters, emails, text messages,
phone calls, voicemails, video conferences, and in-person conversations.

     4. "Person" means any natural person, corporation, partnership, joint
venture, association, governmental entity, or any other legal entity.

     5. "Identify" when used with respect to:
        (a) A person: state the person's full name, last known address,
            telephone number, email address, and relationship to you.
        (b) A document: describe the document's date, author, recipients,
            subject matter, and current location or custodian.
        (c) A communication: state the date, participants, subject matter,
            and whether the communication was oral, written, or electronic.

     6. "Relate to" or "Relating to" means concerning, referring to,
describing, evidencing, or constituting.

     7. "Incident" or "Transaction" means [describe the subject matter of
the litigation].

                              INSTRUCTIONS

     1. These Interrogatories are continuing in nature. You are required
to supplement your responses pursuant to FRCP 26(e) if you obtain
additional information.

     2. If you object to any interrogatory, state the specific grounds for
the objection. If you object to part of an interrogatory, answer the
remaining part.

     3. If you claim privilege, identify the document or communication
withheld, the privilege claimed, and the basis for the privilege.

     4. All interrogatories should be answered based on information known
to you or available to you, including information in the possession of
your attorneys, agents, employees, and representatives.

     5. If an interrogatory cannot be answered in full, answer to the
extent possible and explain why a complete answer cannot be provided.
