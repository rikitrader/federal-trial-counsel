     Rule 37(b)(2)(A) authorizes the following sanctions for failure to
comply with a discovery order:

     (i)   directing that matters be taken as established;
     (ii)  prohibiting the disobedient party from supporting or opposing
           designated claims or defenses;

     (v)   dismissing the action or any part of it;
     (vi)  rendering a default judgment against the disobedient party; or
     (vii) treating as contempt of court the failure to obey any order.

disobedient party to pay reasonable expenses, including attorneys' fees,
unless the failure was substantially justified or other circumstances

## SANCTIONS AUTHORITY COMPARISON

| Authority | Basis | Against Whom | Standard | Sanctions Available |
|-----------|-------|--------------|----------|---------------------|
| **Rule 11** | Frivolous filings | Party and/or counsel | Objective reasonableness | Fees, striking, non-monetary |
| **Rule 37(b)** | Discovery order violation | Party | Willful or grossly negligent | Fees, adverse inference, default |
| **Rule 37(c)** | Disclosure failure | Party | Automatic unless harmless | Exclusion of evidence |
| **Rule 37(e)** | ESI spoliation | Party | Intent (for severe) | Adverse inference, dismissal |
| **§ 1927** | Vexatious litigation | Counsel only | Objective bad faith | Excess fees and costs |
| **Inherent Power** | Bad faith conduct | Party and/or counsel | Subjective bad faith | Fees, dismissal, default |
