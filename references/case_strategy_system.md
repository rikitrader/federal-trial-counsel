## System Classification Criteria (Federal)

### A-System (Aggressive Offense)

**Criteria:**
- Clear federal question or complete diversity established
- Documentary evidence supports all elements
- Twombly/Iqbal plausibility easily satisfied
- No qualified immunity or preemption issues
- Favorable circuit precedent
- Strong damages proof

**Indicators:**
- Win probability: 70%+
- Settlement leverage: High
- Summary judgment: Likely viable under Celotex

### B-System (Balanced Approach)

**Criteria:**
- Jurisdiction secure but contested issues exist
- Mixed facts requiring credibility determinations
- Some circuit splits on legal issues
- Potential qualified immunity defense (§ 1983)
- Expert testimony needed on key issues
- Damages disputed

**Indicators:**
- Win probability: 40-70%
- Settlement leverage: Moderate
- Summary judgment: Uncertain; genuine disputes exist

### C-System (Conservative Defense)

**Criteria:**
- Jurisdictional challenges possible
- Twombly/Iqbal plausibility questionable
- Qualified immunity likely (§ 1983)
- Preemption defense strong
- Unfavorable circuit precedent
- Statute of limitations concerns
- Damages speculative

**Indicators:**
- Win probability: <40%
- Settlement leverage: Low
- Summary judgment: Opponent may obtain
